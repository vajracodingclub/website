Mobile App Development

Introduction

App Inventor lowers the barrier for anyone, anywhere to create mobile apps.
“App Inventor has been a powerful tool for me to develop applications that solve real-world problems.”
“After learning App Inventor, 85% of students agreed that computer science can positively impact their community.”
Unlike other low-code app development platforms, App Inventor supports a range of devices, from the newest iPhone to Android 2.3, a decade-old operating system that is still used by the most under-resourced and vulnerable populations around the world.
App Inventor is a trusted educational resource used by established organizations across the world.

Google Maps, WhatsApp, Instagram have become our everyday essential apps and your child may be the creator of the next BIG thing that you will use in your life. We nurture future leaders in technology by allowing students to explore the path of mobile app development. The MIT App Inventor Platform allows students to create fully functional mobile apps.
With intuitive, visual drag-and-drop programming, our App Inventor curriculum guides students to apply computational thinking and creativity to design their own apps and put them to test immediately on their phones. They can build simple fun apps such as Countdown Timer, Racing Game, Robot Shooter Game and more! Hear about it firsthand from our students who got to present their apps and placed 2nd at the MIT App Inventor Summit (USA).
App Inventor Fundamental's goal is to introduce mobile app programming, instil confidence and an adventurous spirit for students to continue exploring to become little app creators. This course is suitable for students aged 10-12 in the current year.
Lesson 1: Introduction to App Inventor
Learn about the smartphone and its touch interface Explore the App Inventor Interface Load and run mobile Apps
Lesson 2: Events-driven Programming
Understand the event-driven nature of App Inventor programming Learn to use variables and procedures
Lesson 3: Graphics and Animation
Make your animation come alive on your mobile phone Using computational logic (conditional/comparison operators)
Lesson 4: Gaming Time
Learn to put all the knowledge into developing an interactive game on mobile phone
Lesson 5: Project Showcase
Work on your game/app/animation concept Use the techniques you have learned to create your own program Show and tell
